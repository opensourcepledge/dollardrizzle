Dollar Drizzle Dayz

Dollar Drizzle Dayz is a shenanigan from Drizzle, Prettier, SolidStart, Syntax, Sentry, and the Open Source Pledge. Here's where it started.

The Rules

Dollar Drizzle Dayz has simple rules:

  1. Find us at a conference.
  2. Show off.
  3. Receive sweet perks.

The Table of Perks

Tier Shown Off Sweet Perks
1 personal payment of $1/mo to any one Open Source project or maintainer one (1) sweet sticker pack
2 company payments of $1/mo to all (min 100) Open Source dependencies ten (10) sweet sticker packs for you and your colleagues
3 company payments of $1/mo to all (min 100) Open Source dependencies and company membership in the Open Source Pledge ten (10) sweet sticker packs for you and your colleagues, and the consensual physical greeting of your choice‚Ä†

The Point

The point of Dollar Drizzle Dayz is to raise awareness that Open Source maintainers need paid. The Open Source Pledge is a high bar. What's a low bar? $1/mo to your company's direct dependencies. That's probably like $300/mo which is probably what your company spends on granola bars or rice puff snacks or whatever.

GitHub Sponsors

  1. Visit https://github.com/sponsors/explore.
  2. "Explore as" [your org] ... "Apply"
  3. "Download your results as a CSV
  4. Check your primary GitHub email for the CSV attachment.
  5. Run this script to create import files.
  6. Visit https://github.com/sponsors/bulk-sponsorships/import/new?frequency=recurring.
  7. "Sponsor as" [your org]
  8. "Choose file"
  9. Choose the first import file.
  10. You will almost certainly get an error message about minimums. Copy and paste the data from the error message to [your-org]-minimums.csv.
  11. Repeat for all import files in order to fully populate [your-org]-minimums.csv.
  12. Rerun the script to create import files without non-participating maintainers.
  13. Upload the new import files and "Continue to checkout" and "Sponsor NN maintainers" for each.
